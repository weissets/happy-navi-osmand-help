How to find something on the map

  • Find your own position
  • Find Points of Interest (POI)
  • Find by address
  • Find by lat/lon coordinates
  • Find Favorites
  • Find something in your search history

You may want to find the nearest location of a certain type, see your own location or search for a place by zip code. Just use the guide below.

Please note: to accomplish some of these tasks (locate addresses, POI, etc.) you will need to have the offline vector map file.

In the Search option of the Android version, there are five tabs (from left to right):

  • POI (Point Of Interest) search – used for finding specific places of your interest (museums, landmarks, etc.)
  • Address search – consists of four options (Region, City, Street and Building)
  • Location – used for finding a place via its coordinates
  • Favorites search – used for searching your favorite places
  • Search history – self-explanatory
  • You can also move to the section you need by tapping Search POI/Address/Coordinates or History on the dashboard.

In the iOS vresion, there is one Search field used for all types of searches.

Find your own position

Android

Enable GPS or network positioning and wait while the device discovers your position. Once done, you have to press the 'target' button on the right and the map will be zoomed to your area and centered to your location. The light blue circle around you is accuracy of the position detection. You can also enable displaying your current viewing direction, which will follow any device rotation if your device is equipped with compass sensors. You may want to go to Settings - General - Kalman filter to reduce noise in compass readings.

iOS

All you need to do is press the target icon on the main screen and if the app asks for a permission to use the location, press Allow. Finding the position often takes seconds.

Find Points of Interest (POI)

Android

You need pre-loaded vector maps to be able to use this feature.

Go to the dashboard, then tap Search - POI. Next select category or a predefined filter. Select also where you want to search: around your current position, the point currently viewed on the map, a certain other location, etc. After the first search is completed, you may increase the search radius to your needs. To do that, just tap search again (the radius can cover up to 5000 km). Tap on POI to choose Show on Map, Set as Destination, or Show POI description.

Also, if you turn on the Online plugin and download raster maps, you'll be able to search POI by name. This way, your result will be broader. Just type the name of the location and tap Search - Nominatim or Search - Address.

Need to find a public transport? Tap Search POI, then choose Public transport. You can also search for a specifi type of stop. Enter tram stop/bus stop, etc. it the search field to find the stops you need.

iOS

To view POI on the map, tap the Search icon in the upper menu. You'll see a list of POI types. You can tap the type you need to view the list of relevant POI near you. To increase the radius of search, tap Increase search radius. You can do that several times till the message Maximum search radius reached. Please note that the button for increasing radius will be at the bottom of the POI list. To view a certain POI from the list on the map, just tap on it. To see them all on the map, hit the button Show on map at the top of the list. To search for a certain POI, start entering its name into the search.

Find by address

Android

Press Search - POI, then go to Address tab. If you do not have a vector map file for the region in question, you can only perform an online internet search. If you have an offline vector map, simply follow through the guidance by selectively filling the country, city, street, etc. fields. Possible choices of all hits found in your offline data will always be displayed. At any point, you may select Show on Map or Set as Destination. Note: OsmAnd autofills your search when typing, but only on cities and towns. If you type (the beginning of) a village (or smaller) and nothing is displayed, you need to press the Search villages/postcode.

Zipcode/postcode: In the city field start typing the zipcode and when done tap the Search villages/postcode button.

Find by lat/lon coordinates

Android

You can search for a location by its geographic coordinates. To do that, go to the dashboard and choose Search - Coordinates. You'll see the menu where you'll be able to enter the coordinates or choose their format. The coordinates are entered in the format DDD.DD by default, (D means degrees, M - minutes, S - seconds), but you can change that to DDD MM.MM, DDD MM SS.SS, or UTM format. After that, you can navigate to the points (left button on the menu below), choose to set the point as destination (second button from the left), view the location on the map (second button from the right) or add it to the Favorites list (right button). Also, if you tap Set the point as destination (second from the left) button for the second time, you'll be able to replace the destination point if you entered a new set of coordinates, add the location as destination point, add it as first/last waypoint.

iOS

To search for a point determined by geographical coordinates, you just have to enter them into the search field. The coordinates are entered in the formats #.## or ##'##'##.#. This way, you define the latitude and then the longitude of the point. After that, tap Search.

Click Menu button, Specify point, after that you will see the current map coordinates, you can copy or save them and specify other coordinates. The coordinates have different formats, you can choose the preferable one by tapping the Format menu below.

Find Favorites

Android

You can use this feature to find, for example, My Home, address of a friend, etc. First of all, mark any point on the map as favorite (long tap - Add to favorites). After creating favorites you can see your favorite points on the main screen under the button Favorites will be available on the dashboard and in search.

iOS

Enter a query into the Search files. Your favorites will be listed among other results. However, they'll be indicated as favorites.

Find something in your search history

Android

Go to the History tab on your search screen. You will see all search items you searched recently.

iOS

To find something you've searched for before, just enter the name into the Search field. The result of your previous searches will be added to the default result list.
