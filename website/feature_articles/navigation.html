The navigation function allows you to reach your destination easily using voice guidance (optional).

To start the navigation, you need to use the navigation button on the map screen. Next, you'll need to set the starting point and the destination. As for the starting point, you can choose your current location, Favorite location, select a point on the map or use an address. You can also set a starting point by long tapping on the map and choosing 'Set as destination' in the menu box. It is the same with the destination point. As soon as your points are selected, the app will create a route and will start guiding you after you tap Go.

When the navigation is running, you can press the home button. You will get the message 'OsmAnd is runing in the background'. This means that even if you turn the screen off or exit the active app window, you'll still get voice prompts. You can also choose to turn the screen on before turns. To do that, go to Settings — Navigation Settings — Turn screen on and set for how long you'd like to keep the screen on. For instance, if you pick 10 seconds, the screen will be on for 10 seconds on the turn and then switch off again till the next turn.

Navigation profiles

You can pick a car, bicycle or pedestrian profile to build your route. They are calculated according to the traffic rules and will help you reach your destination taking into account your way of movement. To choose the navigation mode, just go to navigation settings bar.

Voice guidance

You can get voice prompts as you move. To get it in your language, go to Settings - General - Voice prompts and choose the language you prefer or press Install more to install the one you need.

How to mute voice prompts

You can mute the voice prompts if you do not need them at the moment. Just tap the sound icon on the navigation menu to stop voice guidance and tap it again to turn it on. The navigation itself will work as usual.

What are TTS and pre-recorded voice prompts

There are two types of voices in OsmAnd: TTS or text-to-speech generated voice prompts and those created from pre-recorded voices. You can switch between them to pick the type that's more comfortable to you. TTS is preferable as it functions in a more flexible way. Choose the voice prompt type you like in Settings — General — Voice prompts — Install more. There, you'll see two gruops of vocie prompts and can pick the option you prefer.

Navigation services

In OsmAnd navigation settings, you can choose the service to calculate your navigation route. The services offered are

  • OsmAnd (offline)
  • "YOURS"
  • OSRM (only car)
  • Straight line

The Straight line option connects your waypoints with a line: it can be used for general trip planning when you do not need a detailed route. Other services have their own algorithms of route calculation, you can test them to see which one meets your needs.

Navigation settings

You can improve up your trip by using specific options of the trip. Tap the Settings button in the navigation window. There, you'll be able to choose Avoid toll roads/unpaved roads/ferries/motorways/border crossing or choose the shortest way. Also, you'll be able to select a GPX route to follow. If you have even more specific requirements to routing, for instance, you want to set your vehicle's height, you can find them in the Navigation settings menu.

How to avoid certain roads

You can customize your route to avoid certain streets or types of roads. To do that, just create a navigation route as usual, then enter the navigation settings in your navigation menu. You can choose to avoid toll roads, unpaved roads, ferries, shuttle train, motorways and border crossing or just select the shortest way. But you can also avoid a certain street. Maybe there are construction works or a traffic jam or anything that makes you want to avoid that section of road. Just tap the button on the right in your navigation settings menu and select the roads to avoid on the map. Your selection will work for current route only — the next time you'll be able to ride that way as usual.

How to add/remove waypoints

To add or remove waypoints, you can just tap on the map after the route is calculated and choose 'Add waypoint'. Add multiple waypoints one by one if needed. To remove a waypoint, just make a long tap on it and choose 'Remove waypoint'.

How to pause, resume or stop navigation

Need to pause your navigation to make a quick stop? That's easy. To pause, resume or stop your navigation completely, go to your dashboard and press the button on your navigation menu next to the Destination info. You can also do the same by tapping on the screen during navigation and pressing the 'x' icon on the left.

How to view POI along the road

You can choose to view points of interest along your route. To do that, please create a new route, then hit the flag icon to see the waypoints menu. Then turn on POI. You'll be able to select the type of POI you'd like to see and also set the radius of search. For example, if you choose 150m and tourism, you'll see all tourist attractions in the radius of 150m around your calculated route.

How to save navigation route as GPX file

You can build a route to use later as a GPX file. This way, you won't have to wait for the route to be calculated and will have a reliable navigation source. To save your current navigation route as a GPX file, go to the navigation menu, then tap the information menu. Tap the Save button in the upper right corner and name your GPX track to save it. To use it for navigation, please go to the dashboard, select the track in your tracks menu, tap Show on map, then press the navigation button.
